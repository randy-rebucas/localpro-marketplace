## Your staff on LocalPro

As an agency owner, you manage a roster of staff members who complete jobs on behalf of your agency. Each staff member has a LocalPro account linked to your agency, which gives you visibility into their assignments, availability, and performance.

## Inviting staff members

1. Go to **Agency Dashboard → Staff**
2. Click **Invite Staff Member**
3. Enter their name and email address
4. Click **Send Invite**

The worker receives an email invitation to create or connect their LocalPro account to your agency. Once accepted, they appear on your Staff page with status **Active**.

> Staff members cannot independently appear in the marketplace — they only work under your agency's profile.

## Staff member capabilities

| Action | Agency Owner | Staff Member |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Submit quotes on behalf of agency | ✅ | ❌ |
| View and accept job assignments | ✅ | ✅ |
| Message clients on assigned jobs | ✅ | ✅ |
| Upload job completion photos | ✅ | ✅ |
| Mark job as complete | ✅ | ✅ |
| View agency earnings and reports | ✅ | ❌ |

## Availability management

Staff members can set their availability from their own account settings. As an agency owner, you can view each worker's current status:

- **Available** — Ready to accept new assignments
- **Busy** — Currently assigned to active jobs
- **Unavailable** — Marked off by the worker or owner (holiday, sick leave, etc.)

When assigning a job, you'll see only available staff listed.

## Tracking staff activity

From the **Staff** page, click on any staff member to view:

- Current and past job assignments
- Completion rate and average client rating
- Total jobs completed (lifetime and current month)
- Any active disputes involving that worker

Use this to identify your top performers and allocate premium jobs accordingly.

## Removing a staff member

To remove someone from your agency:

1. Go to **Staff → [Member Name]**
2. Click **Remove from Agency**
3. Confirm the action

The removed staff member's LocalPro account remains active, but they are no longer linked to your agency. Any jobs that were assigned to them will need to be reassigned. Settle any pending payouts before removing a staff member.

## Handling underperformance

If a staff member consistently receives poor ratings or generates disputes:

- Review their jobs from the Staff Activity panel
- Speak with the worker privately to address issues
- If necessary, reassign future jobs to other staff while the situation is resolved
- Repeated issues can result in the agency's overall rating dropping — use the Remove function if needed

## Tips

- **Keep availability updated** — Assigning a job to an unavailable worker causes delays and damages client relationships.
- **Use names and photos** — Staff profiles with a clear photo and name increase client trust.
- **Check ratings regularly** — Catch low ratings early and address training gaps before they accumulate.
