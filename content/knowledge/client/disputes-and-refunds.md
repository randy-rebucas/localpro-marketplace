## When should I open a dispute?

Open a dispute if:
- Work was not completed as described
- The provider disappeared or stopped responding
- The quality of work is significantly below what was agreed
- There is a disagreement over the scope of work or final price

**Do not** open a dispute as a first step. Always try to resolve the issue by messaging the provider first.

## How to open a dispute

1. Go to **My Jobs** and open the job in question
2. Click **Open Dispute** at the bottom of the job page
3. Select a reason for the dispute
4. Describe the issue clearly — include dates, what was agreed, and what actually happened
5. Attach any supporting evidence (photos, screenshots of messages)
6. Submit

Escrow funds are frozen immediately and will not be released to either party until the dispute is resolved.

## What happens after I submit a dispute?

1. Both you and the provider are notified
2. Our support team reviews the details within 1–2 business days
3. A support agent may ask for additional information from either party
4. A resolution is issued: full refund, partial refund, or full release to provider
5. Both parties are notified of the decision

## Refund outcomes

| Outcome | What happens |
|---------|-------------|
| Full refund | All escrow funds returned to you |
| Partial refund | Agreed portion refunded, remainder released to provider |
| No refund | Full escrow released to provider |

## Tips for a successful dispute

- **Document everything** — take photos of the completed work (or lack thereof)
- **Be factual** — describe what was agreed vs. what was delivered
- **Stay professional** — disputes reviewed more fairly when both parties remain respectful
- **Respond promptly** — delays in responding to our support team can slow resolution

## How long does a dispute take?

Most disputes are resolved within 3–5 business days. Complex cases may take longer if investigation is needed.
