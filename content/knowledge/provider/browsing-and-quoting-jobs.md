## The Marketplace

The **Marketplace** is where all open, validated jobs are listed. You can access it from the **Marketplace** link in your sidebar.

By default, jobs are sorted by most recent. If you have an OpenAI-powered profile, jobs may be ranked by relevance to your skills.

## Filtering jobs

Use the filter bar at the top of the Marketplace to narrow results by:
- **Category** — Filter to your trade (Plumbing, Electrical, Cleaning, etc.)
- **Location** — Focus on your service area
- **Budget** — Filter by the client's listed budget range

## Reading a job listing

Each listing shows:
- Job title and description
- Category and location
- Client's budget
- Number of quotes already submitted
- Time since posting

Click a job card to read the full description before submitting a quote.

## Writing a winning quote

A quote has two parts: **your price** and **your message**.

### Price
Set a price that is fair for the scope of work described. Undercutting significantly may win the job, but often attracts clients who don't value quality. Price confidently based on your experience.

### Message
The message is your chance to stand out. A generic "I can do this job" rarely wins. A strong message includes:

- A brief introduction (your trade, years of experience)
- Acknowledgement of the specific job requirements
- Your approach or method
- Your availability to start
- Any questions you have for the client

**Example:**
*"Hi, I'm a licensed plumber with 6 years of residential experience. I can fix the tap leak you described — this sounds like a worn washer or O-ring, which I can replace in under an hour. I'm available this weekend and can bring all required parts. Do you know if the tap is a mixer or separate hot/cold?"*

## After submitting a quote

- The client will be notified and can view your profile
- You may receive a message from the client with questions
- Respond promptly — fast responses improve your win rate
- The client may accept your quote, decline it, or let it expire

## Quote limits

There is no limit to how many jobs you can quote on simultaneously.
