## Optimise your profile

Your profile is seen by every client before they decide to accept your quote. Small improvements can have a big impact.

### 1. Write a compelling bio
Your bio should answer three questions: What do you do? How long have you been doing it? Why should a client choose you?

Use the **AI Bio Generator** on your profile page to get a strong starting point.

### 2. Add specific skills
Clients search by skill tags. The more relevant skills you have listed, the more searches you appear in. Be specific — "Leak repair" performs better than just "Plumbing."

### 3. Upload portfolio photos
Before-and-after photos of past work are the most compelling proof of quality. Aim for at least 5 photos showing diverse job types.

### 4. Keep availability current
Clients avoid providers marked as unavailable. Update your schedule regularly so you only appear to clients when you can actually take on work.

## Write better quotes

The quote message is your pitch. Most providers send generic messages — standing out is easy.

- **Address the specific job** — mention a detail from the description to show you read it
- **Explain your approach** — clients feel more confident when they understand what you'll do
- **Show credentials** — mention relevant certifications, years of experience, or specialist tools
- **Ask one question** — it shows engagement and starts a conversation

## Respond fast

Speed is a major differentiator. Clients often accept the first quote from a provider who responds within 1–2 hours of the job going live. Enable push notifications to be alerted the moment new jobs match your profile.

## Deliver consistently

The best long-term strategy is simply doing excellent work, every time:

- **Arrive on time** or communicate early if delayed
- **Do exactly what was agreed** — no surprises on price or scope without prior discussion
- **Clean up after yourself** — clients notice and mention it in reviews
- **Ask for a review** after each completed job — politely remind the client it helps your business

## Build your review score

Reviews directly affect your ranking in the marketplace. Providers with 4.8+ stars receive 3–4x more job views than providers with 4.0 stars.

- Focus on the first 5 reviews — they establish your baseline score
- A single 1-star review can be offset by several 5-star reviews over time
- Respond professionally to any negative review — future clients read your response

## Use the calendar

The **Calendar** page helps you avoid overbooking. Mark off days you're unavailable so you don't accidentally accept jobs you can't complete on time. Late or incomplete work is the fastest way to accumulate negative reviews.
