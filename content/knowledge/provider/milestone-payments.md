## How milestone payments work for providers

On large or multi-day jobs, clients can release your payment in stages rather than all at once. Each stage corresponds to a completed phase of the work. You receive each partial payment as soon as the client approves that phase — you don't have to wait until the entire job is done.

## What to do when a milestone is complete

1. Upload **photos** of the completed phase to the job page (strongly recommended)
2. Message the client through the job chat to let them know the phase is done and request partial release
3. The client reviews your photos and clicks **Release Partial Payment** for that phase
4. Funds arrive in your LocalPro balance promptly

## Marking the full job as complete

Once all work is finished:

1. Go to **My Jobs** and open the job
2. Upload your **after-photos** showing the completed work
3. Click **Mark Job Complete**
4. The client receives a notification to review and confirm

The client has **7 days** to confirm completion or open a dispute. If they take no action within 7 days, funds release to you automatically.

## Auto-release timeline

| Day | What happens |
|-----|-------------|
| Day 0 | You mark the job complete |
| Day 1–7 | Client reviews and can confirm or dispute |
| Day 7 | Funds auto-release if no action taken |

## Digital completion confirmation

When the client clicks **Confirm Completion**, you receive a notification and the funds release from escrow immediately. This is the fastest way to get paid — encourage clients to confirm as soon as they're happy with the work.

## Protecting yourself with photos

Photos are your most important evidence. Always upload:

- **Before photos** — condition of the site before you started (add these early in the job)
- **Progress photos** — for milestone projects, after each phase
- **After photos** — completed work from multiple angles

If a client opens a dispute, these photos are reviewed by our support team as evidence of work performed.

## What happens if a client disputes?

If the client opens a dispute:

- Escrow is frozen immediately
- You will be notified and asked to provide your account of what was completed
- Our support team reviews photos, messages, and the job agreement
- A resolution is issued within 3–5 business days

The most common reason disputes go against providers is a lack of documented evidence. Photos and clear job-chat communication are your protection.

## Timeline disputes (late completion)

If you expect to miss an agreed completion date:

- **Communicate early** — message the client through the job chat as soon as you know
- Agree on a revised schedule in writing (via the job chat so it's recorded)
- Avoid going silent — unresponsiveness is the main trigger for auto-dispute escalation

Proactive communication almost always prevents disputes from being raised.

## Partial releases and platform fees

Platform fees apply to each release the same way they apply to a full payment. When a client releases ₱3,000 for a milestone, our platform fee is deducted and the net amount is credited to your balance.
