## The role of PESO verification

Workers enrolled through your office can carry two types of official credentials on their LocalPro profile:

1. **LGU Verification Badge** — confirms the worker was referred and vetted by your PESO office
2. **PESO Certifications** — specific trade or program credentials issued by your office

Both appear as visible badges on the provider's public profile, which increases client trust and improves the worker's win rate on bids.

## LGU Verification Badge

When you refer a worker through the portal, their profile is automatically flagged as pending verification. After you confirm their identity and credentials, you issue the badge.

To verify a worker:

1. Go to **Workforce → Registry**
2. Find the worker (filter by **Pending Verification** status)
3. Click their name to open their profile
4. Review their submitted documents:
   - Valid government-issued ID
   - Barangay certificate (optional but recommended)
   - Any trade license or training certificate they uploaded
5. Click **Verify** to issue the LGU badge, or **Reject** with a note explaining what to resubmit

Verified workers appear with a **"LGU Verified – [Your LGU Name]"** badge on their profile.

## Issuing a PESO Certification

Certifications are specific to a trade, skill, or program. They are issued after a worker completes training or a livelihood program assessment.

To issue a certification:

1. Go to **Workforce → [Worker Name] → Certifications**
2. Click **Issue Certification**
3. Fill in:
   - **Certification name** (e.g., "TESDA NCII Welding — PESO Marikina 2026")
   - **Issuing date**
   - **Expiry date** (if applicable)
   - **Notes** (program batch, assessment score, etc.)
4. Click **Issue**

The certification appears on the worker's public profile with your office name, the certification title, and the validity period.

## Revoking a certification or badge

If a worker's credentials are found to be invalid, or if they are removed from a program due to misconduct:

1. Go to **Workforce → [Worker Name] → Certifications** (or Verification)
2. Click **Revoke** next to the relevant credential
3. Provide a reason — this is recorded internally and not shown publicly
4. Confirm

Revoked certifications are removed from the worker's public profile immediately. The revocation is logged in your office's Activity Log for audit purposes.

## Batch verification

For large program cohorts where all workers have been assessed simultaneously, use the batch verification tool:

1. Go to **Workforce → Bulk Verify**
2. Upload a CSV with worker IDs (or mobile numbers) and certification details
3. Review the preview
4. Click **Issue All** — certifications are issued to all listed workers

## Viewing your office's verified workforce

Go to **Workforce → Registry** and filter by **Verified** status to see all workers currently carrying your office's badge. Use the **Export** button to generate the list for LGU council presentations, DOLE reports, or livelihood program documentation.

## Tips

- **Set expiry dates on trade certifications** — TESDA NCs typically expire after 5 years, and displaying an outdated cert can mislead clients.
- **Use consistent certification names** — standardised names across your office make reports cleaner.
- **Verify promptly** — workers waiting for badge approval cannot market themselves as LGU-verified, which delays their first jobs.
